## Pattern

Assess OBA eligibility against six mandatory criteria before applying; the most common blocker is notability, which requires unpaid editorial coverage in major publications.

## When to Apply

- Customer asks about getting the blue checkmark
- Planning OBA application timeline
- Troubleshooting OBA rejection

## Eligibility Checklist

All six criteria must be met:

1. **Policy Compliance**
   - [ ] Compliant with WhatsApp Business Messaging Policy
   - [ ] No active policy violations on WABA

2. **Platform Tenure**
   - [ ] Registered on platform for at least 30 days
   - [ ] Cannot apply immediately after signup

3. **Notability** (Most common blocker)
   - [ ] Brand covered in major news publications
   - [ ] Coverage must be **editorial, not paid/promotional**
   - [ ] Multiple third-party references preferred

4. **Business Verification**
   - [ ] Portfolio verified through Meta Business Verification
   - [ ] Documents submitted and approved

5. **Two-Step Verification**
   - [ ] 2FA PIN enabled on the phone number
   - [ ] Required for security

6. **Display Name Approved**
   - [ ] Display name verification completed
   - [ ] Must match business identity

## Sub-brand Strategy

For sub-brands lacking independent notability:
- Format: `{{sub-brand}} by {{notable parent}}`
- Example: "Acme Coffee by Acme Corp"
- Leverages parent brand's notability

## Common Rejection Reasons

- Paid content cited as evidence (not valid)
- Insufficient editorial coverage
- Business verification incomplete
- Applied within 30 days of registration

## When NOT to Apply

- Employee accounts (not eligible)
- Test accounts (not eligible)
- WhatsApp Business app numbers (not eligible)
- Just rejected (must wait 30 days before reapplying)

## Examples

- Global brand with Wikipedia page: Likely approved
- Local business, no press coverage: Focus on building notability first
- Sub-brand of Fortune 500: Use "SubBrand by ParentBrand" format
